Banking Fundamentals

Master the principles of modern banking, from personal accounts to international finance systems.

Course Description

This comprehensive banking course covers all aspects of modern banking systems, from basic account management to complex financial instruments. Whether you're preparing for a career in finance or simply want to better manage your personal banking, this course provides practical knowledge.

Key Concept: Fractional Reserve Banking

Banks operate on a fractional reserve system, meaning they only keep a fraction of deposits as reserves and lend out the rest. This system allows banks to create money through the lending process while maintaining liquidity to meet withdrawal demands.

Banking System Overview

The modern banking system is a complex network of institutions that facilitate the flow of money through the economy. Banks serve three primary functions:

Key Concept: Money Creation

Commercial banks create money through the process of lending. When a bank makes a loan, it creates a deposit in the borrower's account, effectively creating new money. This is how most money in modern economies is created.

Types of Banks

Retail Banks

Serve individual consumers with checking/savings accounts, loans, and credit cards.

Commercial Banks

Serve businesses with specialized accounts, loans, and cash management services.

Investment Banks

Help companies raise capital through securities issuance and provide advisory services.

Central Banks

Government institutions that manage monetary policy and regulate the banking system.

Banking Regulations

Banks operate under strict regulations designed to maintain stability and protect consumers. Key regulatory frameworks include:

  • Basel Accords: International banking supervision standards that set capital requirements
  • Dodd-Frank Act: U.S. legislation enacted after the 2008 financial crisis to increase oversight
  • FDIC Insurance: Protects depositors against bank failures (up to $250,000 per account)
  • Anti-Money Laundering (AML): Regulations to prevent financial crimes

Key Concept: Capital Requirements

Banks must maintain minimum capital levels relative to their risk-weighted assets. This ensures they have enough buffer to absorb losses and protects depositors. The current Basel III standard requires a minimum of 8% capital to risk-weighted assets ratio.
